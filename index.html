The Problem

Retail runs on AI now. Governance hasn't caught up.

The EU AI Act is live. Retail companies are deploying AI across pricing, demand forecasting, customer personalisation, and supply chain — but without the governance frameworks, tooling, or in-house specialists to do it responsibly.

01

Compliance is a maze

The AI Act demands risk assessments, documentation, and ongoing monitoring for high-risk systems. Most retail organisations don't know where to start — and consultancies charge a fortune to tell you.

02

Governance slows you down

Without clear frameworks, every AI initiative — from dynamic pricing to automated inventory — gets stuck in review. Teams wait months for approval on systems that should ship in weeks.

03

Experts can't access the tools

Your risk, compliance, and merchandising specialists understand the business problem — but current AI evaluation tools require engineering skills they don't have.

The Platform

Core Capability

Evaluate

Run evaluations and red team your AI systems with a no-code interface built for domain experts — not engineers.

  • Automated red teaming workflows for retail AI (pricing, recommendations, personalisation)
  • Custom evaluation suites for fairness, bias, and safety
  • Domain-expert accessible interface — no Python required
  • Built on the open-source Inspect framework

Comply

Automate documentation and risk assessments for the EU AI Act. Stay audit-ready without the overhead.

  • AI Act compliance documentation for high-risk retail systems
  • Risk management automation (ISO 42001 / NIST AI RMF)
  • System cards and safety reports
  • Audit-ready evidence trails

Innovate

Governance frameworks that enable faster, safer AI adoption across your retail operations — not just compliance for its own sake.

  • Sustainability and carbon tracking for AI workloads
  • ESG reporting for AI systems
  • Tiered metrics: from basic compliance to advanced safety benchmarks
  • Custom safeguards for retail-specific risks (pricing manipulation, discriminatory recommendations, inventory bias)
